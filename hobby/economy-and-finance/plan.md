> Author of plan: Claude. Created: 2026-06-16.

A systematic, foundational course in economics and finance — built from first principles, the way
the main track is, for someone who is **new to the subject as a discipline** but a sharp analyst
(PhD physics; comfortable with quantitative/systems reasoning). You may already hold some of these
concepts in pieces; the goal is to assemble them into one coherent mental model.

---

## What you want out of it (your four goals)

1. **Fully understand economy- and business-related news.**
2. **Understand how global / local economic policy works.**
3. **Be able to digest the financial reports of public companies.**
4. **Understand how investment works and basic strategies.**

The modules below build toward all four; the mapping is called out in [Goal → module](#goal--module).

## How this plan is pitched

- **Foundational and systematic** — concepts in dependency order (micro → macro → policy → markets
  → accounting → analysis → investing), not a grab-bag of tips. Each section earns the next.
- **Concept-first**, like the main track: build the mental model and the vocabulary so you can
  *read and judge* news, policy, and reports — not memorise definitions.
- **Comparative and grounded.** Where there are competing systems (Fed vs MAS, US GAAP vs IFRS,
  active vs passive), the point is the trade-off, not one answer. Examples favour real companies,
  real policy episodes, and real reports.
- **Global-first, with a Singapore/Asia "local" lens** *(default — tell me to change it)*. Your
  goal names *global **and** local* policy; "local" is read as Singapore/Asia (e.g. MAS's unusual
  exchange-rate-based monetary policy, SGX/REITs, SFRS≈IFRS). Investing examples assume a
  Singapore-based investor who can also reach US markets. Redirect if you'd rather centre elsewhere.
- **Bite-sized.** Each **§ section = 1–2 hours**. A "module" is a themed group of sections.

## Modules

### E01 — Economic Foundations (Microeconomics) · `01-foundations/`
*The grammar everything else is written in: how prices, markets, and firms work.*
- §1 — How economists think: scarcity, incentives, opportunity cost, marginal thinking, trade-offs.
- §2 — Supply, demand & how prices coordinate a market.
- §3 — Elasticity, surplus, and when markets fail (externalities, public goods, monopoly power).
- §4 — Firms, costs & competition: how a business actually decides what to make and charge.
  *(Strategic/oligopoly competition — Cournot, Bertrand — is treated properly in the sibling
  [Game Theory](../game-theory/plan.md) subject; this section just flags where it bites.)*

### E02 — The Whole Economy (Macroeconomics) · `02-macroeconomics/`
*Reading "the economy" — the indicators behind the headlines. (Goal 1.)*
- §1 — GDP & measuring output: what "the economy grew 2%" really means (and what it misses).
- §2 — Inflation & price indices: CPI, real vs nominal, why a little inflation is the target.
- §3 — Unemployment & the labour market: the headline rate, participation, wages.
- §4 — The business cycle: booms, recessions, leading indicators, why downturns happen.

### E03 — Money, Banking & Monetary Policy · `03-money-and-central-banks/`
*Where most economic-policy news lives. (Goal 2.)*
- §1 — What money is, and how banks create it (credit, fractional reserves, the money supply).
- §2 — Interest rates & the time value of money: the price of money, the yield curve.
- §3 — Central banks & monetary policy — the standard (Fed) model: setting rates, inflation
  targeting, quantitative easing, the transmission mechanism.
- §4 — The MAS model: Singapore's **exchange-rate-based** monetary policy — why it's unusual, and
  the comparison that makes the standard model click *(local lens)*.

### E04 — Government & the Public Finances (Fiscal Policy) · `04-fiscal-policy/`
*The other half of policy. (Goal 2.)*
- §1 — Taxes, spending & the government budget: where money comes from and goes.
- §2 — Deficits, public debt & sustainability: when debt matters, when it doesn't.
- §3 — The policy mix: fiscal + monetary together — stimulus, austerity, and their limits.

### E05 — The Global Economy · `05-global-economy/`
*Trade, currencies, and capital across borders. (Goals 1 & 2.)*
- §1 — Trade & comparative advantage: why countries trade, tariffs, supply chains.
- §2 — Exchange rates & the balance of payments: what moves a currency, current vs capital account.
- §3 — Capital flows, crises & globalization — with Singapore as a trade/finance hub *(local lens)*.

### E06 — Financial Markets & Instruments · `06-markets-and-instruments/`
*The bridge: what's actually being bought, sold, and reported on. (Goals 3 & 4.)*
- §1 — What financial markets are, who's in them, and what they're for (primary vs secondary).
- §2 — Stocks (equity): what a share legally is, how ownership and dividends work.
- §3 — Bonds & fixed income: lending to companies and governments, price vs yield.
- §4 — A tour of the rest: money markets, FX, commodities, and derivatives (what they're for).

### E07 — Accounting & Reading Financial Statements · `07-financial-statements/`
*The core of goal 3 — read a company's books without taking management's word for it. (Goal 3.)*
- §1 — The accounting equation & double-entry: the foundation that makes the statements tie out.
- §2 — The income statement: revenue → costs → profit, and its margins.
- §3 — The balance sheet: what a company owns, owes, and is worth (book value).
- §4 — The cash flow statement: why profit ≠ cash, and how the three statements link together.
- §5 — Notes, accounting choices & red flags: accrual vs cash, revenue recognition, where the
  bodies are buried.

### E08 — Analysing a Company · `08-company-analysis/`
*From "read the report" to "judge the business." (Goal 3.)*
- §1 — Ratio analysis: profitability, liquidity, leverage, efficiency — and what each reveals.
- §2 — Common-size & trend analysis; how to actually read an annual report / 10-K.
- §3 — Valuation basics: multiples (P/E, EV/EBITDA…) vs intrinsic value (DCF intuition); price ≠ value.

### E09 — Investing: First Principles · `09-investing-principles/`
*The why before the how. (Goal 4.)*
- §1 — Risk, return & compounding: the time value of money, doing the maths on growth.
- §2 — Diversification & portfolio thinking: why the portfolio, not the pick, is the unit.
- §3 — Asset classes & how they behave: equities, bonds, cash, property/REITs, alternatives.
- §4 — Are markets efficient? Efficient-market ideas, bubbles, and behavioural finance.

### E10 — Investment Strategies & Putting It Together · `10-investment-strategies/`
*The how, and a personal wrap. (Goal 4.)*
- §1 — Passive vs active; index funds & ETFs (and why costs dominate long-run returns).
- §2 — Asset allocation: building a long-term portfolio and rebalancing it.
- §3 — Strategy styles: value, growth, dividend/income, dollar-cost averaging — and their trade-offs.
- §4 — Costs, taxes, behavioural traps & a personal-finance wrap *(SG flavour optional: CPF, SRS, REITs)*.
